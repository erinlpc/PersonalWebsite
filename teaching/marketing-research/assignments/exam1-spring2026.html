Intro, outline, and dataset generator

The Scenario

You’ve just started a new role as a data analyst at Coastal Maine Properties, a regional real estate development company. Your boss, the Chief Marketing Officer, has asked you to analyze data from 100 recent home sales to inform pricing strategy for a new development.

The company has historically used simple “rules of thumb” based on assumptions about the typical home sale for their simple (or compact) models:

Variable Company Assumption
Home price $220,000
Buyer age 33 years old
Buyer income $83,000

Your job is to evaluate how well these assumptions hold up against actual sales data — and to quantify how much better you could predict outcomes using data-driven estimates.

Your manager is sharp but not a statistician. Throughout this exam, you’ll need to translate your findings into language he can use for decision-making. He’ll ask questions like:

  • “How wrong are our current assumptions?”
  • “How much better do we really do with your data-driven approach?”
  • “How confident should we be in these estimates?”
  • “What would it take to be more certain?”

Part 1: Price

This section asks you to perform a complete analysis of the Price variable. You’ll compare the performance of the model that uses the company’s normal assumption ($220,000) against your data-driven (augmented) model estimates.

What You’ll Calculate

  • Model A predictions (using mean and median)
  • Error metrics: SSE and SAE for Model C and Model A (using mean & median)
  • PRE under both SSE and SAE
  • Which measure of center performs better under each error function
  • Inference statistics: SSR, MSR, MSE, RMSE, F statistic, p-value
  • Effect size: Cohen’s f²
  • Power at α = 0.05
  • 95% Confidence Interval for PRE

What You’ll Interpret

  • Why mean vs. median perform differently
  • Whether to reject the company’s assumption
  • What the effect size tells us about practical significance
  • What power and the CI tell us about certainty and precision

Key Formulas to Remember

Statistic Formula
SSE \(\sum(y - \hat{y})^2\)
SAE \(\sum|y - \hat{y}|\)
PRE \((Error_C - Error_A) / Error_C\)
Cohen’s f² \(PRE / (1 - PRE)\)
F statistic \((PRE / df_1) / ((1 - PRE) / df_2)\)

Where \(df_1 = 1\) and \(df_2 = n - 1\) for our simple model comparison.

You should have functions for power and CI from Week 6 exercises.

Part 4: Interpretation

This section asks you to compare model performance across your analyses.

I will ask you to

  • Comparing PRE and effect sizes across variables
  • Interpreting power in plain language for a manager
  • Understanding what affects power (sample size, effect size, alpha)
  • Estimating sample size needed for adequate power
  • Interpreting confidence intervals for decision-making
  • Understanding SESOI (Smallest Effect Size of Interest)
  • Distinguishing statistical vs. practical significance

Then prepare to Explain (in plain language to your boss)

  • “What does power actually mean?”
  • “What could we do to increase power?”
  • “What does a wide confidence interval mean for our decisions?”
  • “Why should we care about anything other than whether or not we have a significant p-value?”

TipTips for Managerial Communication

  • Be clear about certainty and limitations
  • Make actionable recommendations

General Tips

  1. START EARLY. This exam will likely take most students on average between 4-8 hours of focused work. Don’t wait until Sunday to start.

  2. WORK THROUGH PREVIOUS EXERCISES. Everything on this exam builds on Weeks 1-6 exercises. Reference them.

  3. CHECK YOUR WORK. If you want, use the sample answer key (seed 123456) to verify your code works correctly before beginning the exam on Brightspace.

  4. THINK BEFORE YOU CALCULATE. Make sure that you understand what each statistic means, not just how to compute it.

  5. TRANSLATE FOR YOUR AUDIENCE. When I ask you to explain, do so without jargon. Remember, your manager doesn’t know what PRE means.

  6. ASK QUESTIONS WISELY. Remember the “tell me 3 things you tried” policy. Try to solve problems yourself first, document what you tried, then I will be happy to help where I can.
